Why your hook is the only metric that matters

TikTok's retention graph drops the steepest between second 1 and second 3. Move a viewer past that window and your average watch time doubles; lose them, and the algorithm buries the rest of your episode no matter how cinematic the visuals are. Signed writers at ReelShort, DramaBox and PineDrama describe the same rule: the hook is 70% of the win.

The 4 levers that make a 3-second hook land

  1. Status violation — low-status character out-ranks a high-status one in one sentence ("Fire her." "Sir, she owns this building.").
  2. Identity reveal — the person the protagonist insulted turns out to be the only person who can save them.
  3. Time pressure — you add a clock: marriage in 48 hours, eviction tomorrow, contract signs at noon.
  4. Unanswered question — end the first clip on a question the viewer must swipe for ("Why is my ex-husband at my daughter's school?").

How to use the generator

  1. Pick the trope closest to your niche. If you cross tropes (werewolf + revenge), generate each separately then merge the best beats manually.
  2. Write the setup in one sentence — who is the protagonist right now before the inciting incident.
  3. Pick the target audience. English-speaking 18-34 female tolerates more explicit wealth markers; LATAM and SEA audiences respond more to family/honor stakes.
  4. Generate, rank, and A/B test 2 to 3 hooks per video. Creators who ship the top-30% hook consistently outperform creators with one "perfect" hook.
