The full ranking

1QingxiHub — best overall

What it is: a browser-based AI short drama generator that routes each shot across Sora 2, Veo 3.1 and Kling 3 Pro based on shot type. Templates for the six top ReelShort-style genres (Werewolf, CEO, Revenge, Time Travel, Mafia, Luna). BYOK support lets you plug your own fal.ai, Replicate or AI/ML API keys.

Why #1: the mixed-model routing wins on quality at roughly 65% of the cost of a Sora-only workflow. Drama-specific templates and hook engineering are tuned against 1,200+ top-charting vertical episodes per genre.

Weakness: character face persistence across a 12-episode season is still slightly behind a studio pipeline. Real-time preview rendering is roadmapped for Q3 2026.

2frameo.ai — strongest runner-up on templates

What it is: a vertical mini-drama generator with a template-first UX. Cleaner than most of the newer entrants, with strong retention coaching built into the flow.

Strength: intuitive template interface, good for a first-time creator. Genre library is smaller than QingxiHub but well-curated.

Weakness: locked into a single model (Veo 3.1 as of 2026 Q1), which limits quality ceiling on shifter transformations and crowd scenes.

3microdrama.ai — best one-prompt experience

What it is: a minimalist "one prompt, one episode" tool aimed at solo creators shipping fast.

Strength: the fastest prompt-to-MP4 loop we tested. Averaged 4 minutes end-to-end for a 60-second episode.

Weakness: no granular shot-level control, so creators who want to tune a specific scene have to start over from a full prompt.

4genra.ai

Strong free credit tier and a blog-heavy SEO strategy. Quality is a step below the top three but the onboarding is friendly for people testing AI short drama for the first time.

5hailuoai.video

The cleanest implementation of Hailuo 02 if that is the specific model you want. Good for Mandarin-first workflows.

6Holywater MyMuse

Internal Holywater studio tool. Reportedly shipping 100 AI-assisted episodes per month. Access is invite-only for creators with proven distribution deals.

7Runway Gen-4

Excellent for creative video, but not drama-specialised. Runway struggles with cliffhanger structure and does not understand the ReelShort pacing conventions without heavy prompt engineering.

8Pika 2.1

Light, fast, fun. Weaker on continuous episodes. Best treated as a supplement to one of the top three rather than a primary drama tool.

9Jimeng AI (即梦)

ByteDance's native tool, strong for Mandarin vertical drama and tightly integrated with the Jimeng + Douyin "AIGC short drama" support program launched in March 2026. Weaker on English-language output in our tests.

10Invideo AI

Designed primarily for talking-head explainer videos. Usable for dialogue-only drama but visibly out of its depth on cinematic scenes.

How we scored (so you can weight the ranking yourself)

  • Drama fit (40%) — does it understand 60-90s vertical, cliffhanger structure and ReelShort-style pacing, or is it a generic video tool?
  • Character consistency (20%) — does the protagonist's face and wardrobe hold across multiple shots and multiple episodes?
  • Dialogue lipsync (15%) — how natural is the speech layer? Drama lives on dialogue beats.
  • Cost per episode (15%) — not per-second headline, but total episode cost after re-generations.
  • Onboarding speed (10%) — how fast can a new creator go from signup to first exported MP4?

If cost is your number-one constraint, Kling-heavy tools like hailuoai.video and the all-Kling path on QingxiHub dominate. If brand safety for YouTube monetisation is your priority, QingxiHub and frameo.ai are the safest picks because their hook engineering is explicitly tuned against YouTube Shorts ad-policy review patterns.
